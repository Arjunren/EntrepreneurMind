Key Activities

What key activities do the following require?

  • Automatically detects when items are used removed from the container using an active weight-based tracking system.
  • Automatically records all transactions and exports the data to Excel.
  • Triggers notifications or draft orders via n8n when stock reaches a critical minimum.

Our value propositions

  • Removes the need for manual scanning by actively tracking physical products through weight.
  • Removes the risks of miscalculated stock levels or forgotten entries.
  • Prevents lost sales by ensuring best-selling products remain in stock through real-time monitoring and low-stock alerts.
  • Makes accounting easier by providing reports on monthly profit and inventory usage.

Our distribution channels

  • Allows online stock monitoring for our esteemed customers, using an ESP32 module for data synchronization.
  • Connects directly with trusted digital marketplaces via Shopify to make automated inventory management efficient.

Our customer relationships

  • Actively notifies the customer whenever stock reaches a critical minimum threshold.
  • Constant Monitoring

Customer Relationships

How do we get, keep, and grow customers?

  • We maintain trust through a reliable web-based interface and automated alerts that ensure business owners never miss a sale.
  • We scale with our clients by offering hardware IoT kits and automation features as their business expands
  • We attract users by demonstrating how an "input-free" system solves the headache of manual data entry and stockouts.

Which customer relationships have we with the rest of our business model

  • We provide direct support during the initial setup and configuration to ensure the system integrates smoothly with the client's current workflow.
  • We provide a user-friendly dashboard that allows business owners to access and manage their own data independently at any time.

How costly are they?

  • Setup Phase is our most resource-heavy phase, involving a one-time fee for physical installation, hardware configuration, and integrating the system into the client's current workflow
  • Support are the ongoing costs that are tied to maintaining the web-based interface and ensuring the ESP32 modules stay synced with the cloud
  • As a customer "grows," costs are associated with providing additional IoT equipment or expanding their API integrations (like Shopify or n8n) to handle more inventory

Channels

Through which channels do our customer segments want to be reached?

  • Business owners need a remote, 24/7 digital dashboard to monitor stock levels without being physically present at the store.
  • Email notifications are preferred for urgent updates, such as low-stock warnings or daily profit reports.

How do other companies reach them now?

  • Traditional suppliers often rely on physical visits or phone calls to take reorders.
  • Software companies typically use email marketing and in-app notifications for standard inventory management software.

Which ones work best?

  • Showing the "weight-based" automation in real-time is the most effective way to build trust with non-technical business owners.

Which ones are most cost-efficient?

  • Using the ESP32 module for data transmission is much cheaper than manual auditing or hiring staff for data entry.
  • Providing a web interface reduces the need for constant customer support calls, as owners can check their own data anytime.

Cost Structure

What are the important costs inherent to our business model?

  • Buying the main components like the ESP32 boards, load cells, and HX711 amplifiers.
  • Small recurring costs for cloud hosting or any automation tools (like n8n) that keep the data syncing 24/7.

Which key resources are the most expensive?

  • The ESP32 microcontrollers are the "brains" of the operation, they are more powerful than basic boards and handle all our Wi-Fi processing.
  • High-quality load cells and HX711 modules that ensure the weight readings are accurate every time.

Which key activities are the most expensive?

  • Every shop is different, so the initial labor of mounting the hardware and tweaking the code for each client is a major resource draw.
  • Making sure the ESP32 communicates perfectly with the web ledger and stays connected without dropping out.

Revenue Streams

For what value are our customers willing to pay?

  • Automation & Convenience
  • Accuracy & Reliability
  • Operational Time Savings

For what do they currently pay?

  • Customers currently pay personnel assigned to manually monitor stocks
  • Additional labor costs are incurred due to the need for dedicated employees
  • Employees are responsible for tracking inventory manually
  • Staff must also handle the process of placing orders when stock runs low

What is the revenue model?

  • Revenue from the initial sale of the IoT equipments (ESP32) and others (Load cells/HX711).
  • A one-time service charge for the on-site installation, calibration, and integration of the hardware into the client's shop.

What are the pricing tactics?

  • The customer pays a single, larger upfront fee to own the hardware (ESP32 kits) and the perpetual license to use the software.
  • Offering fixed-term contracts (e.g., 1-year or 2-year terms) that bundle the hardware, software access, and maintenance into an agreement.
